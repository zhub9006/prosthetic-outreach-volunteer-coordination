# Clinical Trials — Affordable Prosthetic Solutions

## Tracked Studies

### 1. NCT04725461 — Low Cost Prosthetic Socket for Lower Limb Amputees
- **Sponsor**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab
- **Status**: Completed
- **Focus**: Expanded rigid foam socket fabrication system for transtibial prostheses in developing countries
- **Key Innovation**: Simplified socket fitting protocol using expandable rigid foam (mixing two liquid components), drastically reducing cost and manufacturing complexity
- **Relevance**: Directly addresses affordable lower-limb prosthetic access in resource-limited settings
- **Tags**: Low-cost, Lower Limb, 3D Manufacturing, Developing Countries

### 2. NCT03517774 — Improving Prosthetic Care for Lower Limb Amputees (3DPrintAbility)
- **Sponsor**: Sunnybrook Health Sciences Centre / Nia Technologies
- **Status**: Completed
- **Focus**: 3D-printed transtibial prosthetic sockets; digital manufacturing tool-chain
- **Key Innovation**: Reduces fitting time from 5 days to 1.5 days while maintaining equivalent quality; tested in Cambodia, Tanzania, Uganda before expanding to North American adult rehab
- **Relevance**: Proven technology for rapid, affordable prosthetic socket fabrication
- **Tags**: 3D Printing, Rapid Fabrication, Global Health, Cost Reduction

### 3. NCT05220553 — PROLIMB: Sensorimotor Prosthesis for the Upper Limb
- **Sponsor**: University Hospitals Coventry and Warwickshire NHS Trust
- **Status**: Completed
- **Focus**: Affordable body-powered prosthetic fingertip digit with integrated haptic feedback
- **Key Innovation**: Body-powered design (~£1200 vs. £20,000-£75,000 for myoelectric) with tactile sensory feedback; addresses the fact that only 2% of upper limb amputees in low-income countries have access to prosthetic care
- **Relevance**: Demonstrates cost-effective alternatives to expensive myoelectric prosthetics
- **Tags**: Upper Limb, Body-Powered, Haptic Feedback, Affordability, Global Access

### 4. NCT07437664 — AI-Powered 3D-Printed Robotic Hand for Transradial Amputations
- **Sponsor**: Al-Nahrain University
- **Status**: Active (not recruiting)
- **Focus**: Low-cost, AI-powered prosthetic system for transradial amputations using 3D-printed sockets
- **Key Innovation**: 3D scanning for custom sockets + AI-driven EMG pattern recognition for adaptive grasping; low-cost manufacturing
- **Relevance**: Directly aligned with affordable prosthetics through AI and 3D printing
- **Tags**: AI, 3D Printing, Upper Limb, EMG, Low-Cost

### 5. NCT07031999 — Biocompatible Thermoplastic Impregnated With Vancomycin (PLA Spacer)
- **Sponsor**: Hospital Universitario Evangelico de Curitiba / PUCPR
- **Status**: Not yet recruiting
- **Focus**: Biocompatible, 3D-printed PLA orthopedic spacer with antibiotic impregnation for periprosthetic infections
- **Key Innovation**: Low-cost, customizable 3D-printed implant addresses high cost of bone substitutes in public healthcare systems
- **Relevance**: Addresses cost of implant components in revision prosthetic surgery
- **Tags**: 3D Printing, PLA, Antibiotic, Orthopedic, Low-Cost

### 6. NCT07519746 — Satisfaction and Quality of Life Among Prosthetic Users in Gaza
- **Sponsor**: Yeditepe University / Al-Azhar University - Gaza
- **Status**: Completed
- **Focus**: Patient satisfaction and quality of life in a conflict-affected region with limited prosthetic access
- **Key Innovation**: Evaluates care delivery models in resource-constrained settings; informs context-specific interventions for affordable prosthetic care
- **Relevance**: Provides framework for outreach in underserved and conflict-affected regions
- **Tags**: Conflict Zone, Access, Quality of Life, Global Outreach
