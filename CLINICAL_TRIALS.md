# Clinical Trials — Affordable Prosthetic Solutions

## Tracked Studies

### 1. NCT07437664 — AI-Powered 3D-Printed Robotic Hand
- **Sponsor**: Al-Nahrain University
- **Status**: Active (not recruiting)
- **Focus**: Low-cost, AI-powered prosthetic system for transradial amputations using 3D-printed sockets
- **Key Innovation**: Customizable, low-cost manufacturing via 3D scanning and printing
- **Keywords**: Low-cost Prosthetics, 3D Printing, Artificial Intelligence
- **Relevance**: Directly aligned with affordable prosthetics goal

### 2. NCT00888199 — Tensegrity K3 Promoter Prosthetic Foot
- **Sponsor**: Tensegrity Prosthetics (NIH collaboration)
- **Status**: Unknown (previously not yet recruiting)
- **Focus**: K3 Promoter foot prosthesis — reduces metabolic cost of transport, improves stability
- **Key Innovation**: Tensegrity structure for efficient energy transfer
- **Relevance**: Cost-effectiveness research for prosthetic feet

### 3. NCT04059107 — 3D Printed Myoelectric Prosthetics for Children
- **Sponsor**: Oregon Health and Science University
- **Status**: Enrolling by invitation
- **Focus**: Evaluation of 3D printed myoelectric prosthetics in ulnar congenital limb deficiency
- **Key Innovation**: Low-cost 3D-printed prosthetics for pediatric patients
- **Relevance**: Pediatric affordable prosthetics
